Empowering Survivors, Building Communities

Project Maaman is a community-centered initiative funded by the Projects for Peace program. It is dedicated to supporting individuals in need, with a special focus on sexual assault survivors in rural Alsweida, Syria. Our mission is to provide safe spaces, access to mental health resources, and skill-building opportunities, empowering survivors to rebuild their lives and fostering resilience within their communities.

While our primary focus is on survivors of sexual violence, our programs extend to anyone in need of support, ensuring inclusivity and accessibility. For sexual assault survivors, we offer an anonymous reporting platform, specialized counseling, and workshops to raise community awareness and combat stigma.

As part of the Projects for Peace initiative, Project Maaman is driven by the goal of creating sustainable change. By addressing the unique challenges of rural areas, we aim to not only support individuals but also foster peace and understanding within the broader community, paving the way for a future built on empathy and hope
